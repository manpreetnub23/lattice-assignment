Selection Test: Weather Dashboard
Role: Junior ReactJS Frontend Developer
Project Overview
The goal of this test is to develop a responsive web application that integrates with the Open-Meteo API (https://open-meteo.com) to provide real-time and historical weather insights. The application must automatically detect the user's location via browser GPS upon landing to display localized data.
Technical Requirements
Framework: ReactJS.
Data Source: Open-Meteo API.
Performance: The application must load and render all data within 500 milliseconds.
Responsiveness: The UI and all data visualizations must be fully mobile-friendly.
Feature Breakdown
Page 1: Current Weather & Hourly Forecast
This page should display information for the current date by default or a specific date selected via a calendar view.
1. Individual Weather Variables: Display the following parameters clearly as individual values:
Temperature: Minimum, Maximum, and Current.
Atmospheric Conditions: Precipitation , Relative Humidity , and UV Index.
Sun Cycle: Sunrise and Sunset times.
Wind & Air: Maximum Wind Speed and Precipitation Probability Max.
Air Quality Metrics: Air Quality Index , PM10 , PM2.5 , Carbon Monoxide (CO) , Carbon Dioxide (CO2) , Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2) , and Sulphur Dioxide (SO2).
2. Hourly Data Visualizations: Create individual graphs for each bullet point below, plotted at hourly intervals for the selected date:
Temperature. (User can toggle from centigrade to fahrenheit)
Relative Humidity.
Precipitation.
Visibility.
Wind Speed (10m).
PM10 & PM2.5 (Combined in a single graph).
Page 2: Historical Date range (Max 2 years)
A separate navigation view where users can analyze long-term trends by selecting a date range (up to a maximum of 2 years).
Historical Charts: The developer must choose the most suitable chart type (line, bar, etc.) based on the nature of the variables.
Temperature: Mean, Max, and Min.
Sun Cycle: Sunrise and Sunset (displayed in IST).
Precipitation: Total values for the range.
Wind: Max Wind Speed and Dominant Wind Direction.
Air Quality: PM10 and PM2.5 trends.
Visualizations & UI Standards
All graphs and charts must include the following interactive features:
Horizontal Scrolling: For navigating dense datasets.
Zoom Functionality: Ability to zoom in and out of specific timeframes.
Mobile Adaptation: Components must automatically adjust to remain legible on mobile screens.
